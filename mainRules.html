Fleet phrase:

Each player inform the DM/ bookKeeper one of the below action in secret. There will be a microsoft form for this every week.

1. Expand on a specific island
2. Blockade mainland port
3. Defend

1. Expand on a specific island

If the island is unoccupied:
- Only one player expands on it: that player gains the island automatically.
- More than two players attempt to expand: nothing happens, as all parties retreat when there are multiple opponents.
- Exactly two players attempt to expand on the same island a Man o' War naval battle occurs.

If the island is already occupied:
- Only one player attempts to attack: a naval battle occurs.
- Two players attempt to attack: they battle each other; the winner receives a set amount of gold.
- Three or more players attempt to attack: nothing happens.

2. Blockade Mainland port

- If the defending player chooses to defend the specified port, a naval battle occurs.
- If the defender does not defend, roll a D6 to determine blockade effects (refer to Blockade Table).
- If two or more players choose to blockade the same port, roll dice to determine who attacks. The winner of the roll fights the defender in a naval battle.

3. Defend

The player may station their fleet in a port of their choice for the turn. Unless they lose a blockade battle that same turn, that port cannot be the target of a port-to-port land battle (see Mainland Phase for details).

Note: All Man o' War battles can be auto-resolved if there is not enough time during the week for a full game.

Auto-Resolve:

- Dice duel: best of 5 rounds, D6 vs D6.
- If both players agree to a draw, nothing happens.

Mainland Phase:

Each player inform the DM/ bookKeeper one of the below action in secret. There will be a microsoft form for this every week.

1. Attack a bordering province
2. Attack a coastal province from one of your own ports
3. If you have no valid attack targets, you may raid any player not already engaged in a 1v1 battle (Chaos and Skaven players may spawn to raid any player)

1. Attack a bordering province

- Declare which province you are attacking from.
- Both attacker and defender benefit from castles and outposts.
- If the attacker loses, they lose the attacking province.
- If the defender loses, they lose the defending province.

2. Attack a coastal province from one of your own ports

- Declare the attacking province.
- Both attacker and defender benefit from castles and outposts.
- The target province must not have a defending fleet stationed, unless a naval blockade was won that turn.
- If the attacker loses, they lose the attacking province.
- If the defender loses, they lose the defending province.

3. Raid

- Only the defender benefits from castle/outpost bonuses.
- The attacker gains gold based on the victory type.
- On a major victory by the attacker, the attacker may destroy one or all buildings.
- On a major victory by the defender, they gain additional gold.

Special Raiding Rules for Skaven, Warriors of Chaos, Chaos Daemons:

- May raid as a standard battle option without restrictions.
- May spawn and attack any province.
- If they win, all buildings are destroyed.
- Gain 1 VP for successful raiding and +1 VP if a castle is destroyed.
- The defender retains control of the province.

Special Rules for Beasts, Orcs, Ogres:

- On a close victory, destroy all buildings in the target province.
- Gain 1 VP if a castle is destroyed this way.

Special Rules for High Elves, Wood Elves:

-Each time achieving a major victory, a hero of the players choice roll a d6 for the elve table (Temp buff for next turn)

Note: All land battles can be auto-resolved if there is not enough time during the week for a full game or players cannot show up to the game.

Auto-Resolve:

- Dice duel: best of 5 rounds, D6 vs D6.
- If both players agree to a draw, nothing happens.

End of Turn Phase:

1. XP allocation
2. Battle Scar allocation
3. Each player discuss among themselves whether they want to form/break alliances
4. Each player discuss among themselves whether they want to form truce 5. Determine Victory Points

Alliance rule

- Max 1 alliance - Alliance: lasts until one side breaks it.

Truce rule

- Max 2 - lasts until the start of the next After Battle Phase.
